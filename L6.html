MEDIA

Media plays a crucial role in forming public awareness, spreading information, and influencing public opinion and attitudes. It can be found on the internet, radio, and television, and has become a powerful tool. Media educates, informs, and entertains through news, features, and analysis. It also produces documentaries, dramas, current affairs programs, public service announcements, and other types of radio and television programming.

Traditional Media

Traditional media refers to advertising channels that have been used for decades. Businesses have relied on these methods, which can be very powerful and sometimes make up the entire advertising budget. Examples include TV commercials, radio ads, cold calling, flyers, billboards, door-to-door sales, and newspaper ads. Although these channels have been important for years, their influence has been steadily declining in recent times.

Electronic Media

Electronic media refers to media that allows users to access content through electronic or electromechanical devices. Unlike traditional print media, which is static, electronic media is interactive and accessed digitally. Examples include video and audio recordings, multimedia presentations, slide presentations, CD-ROMs, and internet content. With the rise of the internet, people can stream music, share files, and access educational or entertainment content easily on computers and mobile devices.

Benefits to the society

Electronic media has brought many benefits to society. It eliminates the barriers of distance and time, making it easier to share information, events, and ideas. People now have simple and widespread access to information, and sharing it has become less expensive and easier. Two-way communication is now simple, allowing people to exchange thoughts and ideas. The broad reach and low cost of electronic media help spread information widely. In politics, electronic media allows equal opportunities for all parties to raise issues, supporting a more democratic exchange of ideas.

History of Radio

The history of radio began with Morse code, developed by Samuel F.B. Morse, which used dots and dashes to send messages and urgent distress signals. Heinrich Hertz, a German physicist, studied radio waves and showed that signals could be transmitted wirelessly, with frequency measured in hertz. Guglielmo Marconi, an Italian inventor, created a device in 1896 that could ring a bell from 30 feet away, marking an early step in wireless communication. By 1916, radio was used to broadcast news, and by 1927 it had become a key source of information. Radio broadcasting in India started in 1923–1924 with radio clubs in Bombay, Calcutta, and Madras. During World War II, radio transmission gained even greater importance.

Significance of Electronic Media

The significance of electronic media lies in its ability to share information quickly and widely, providing real-time news and access to diverse content. It enhances communication by connecting people and enabling interactive exchanges. In education, it supports e-learning and easy access to resources, while in entertainment, it offers various formats and streaming services. Electronic media also promotes cultural exchange by reaching global audiences, drives social change through advocacy and citizen journalism, boosts the economy via e-commerce and advertising, and encourages political engagement by informing citizens and supporting campaigns.
