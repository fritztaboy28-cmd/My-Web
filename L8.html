Legal, Ethical and Societal Issues

Legal, ethical, and societal issues are important in technology and software development. Legal issues deal with following laws, rules, and policies about technology, data, and intellectual property. Ethics focus on what is morally right or fair, even if it’s not required by law. Societal issues consider how technology affects communities, culture, economies, and people’s well-being. Understanding these issues helps create technology that is fair, safe, and trusted.

COPYRIGHT

Copyright is a legal protection that gives the creator of a work—like a book, music, or artwork—the exclusive right to publish, sell, and control how it is used. The creator can allow others, such as publishers or record companies, to use the work, usually for payment. If someone uses the work without permission, it is called copyright infringement.

PLAGIARISM

The act of adopting or closely replicating the language and concepts of another author without permission; the portrayal of that author's work as one's own, such as by failing to acknowledge the source.

CYBER BULLYING

Cyberbullying is bullying that happens through electronic devices like phones, laptops, or tablets, and through online platforms such as social media, emails, or chat rooms. It can include sending hurtful messages, spreading rumors, sharing embarrassing images or videos, or creating fake accounts to humiliate someone.

PHISHING

Phishing is when someone tries to steal sensitive information like usernames, passwords, or credit card details by pretending to be a trusted source online. This is usually done through fake emails, websites, or messages, and is a common method used in cybercrime.

DIGITAL PIRACY

Digital piracy is the illegal copying and selling of digital content like music, movies, software, and other media. It violates copyright laws, hurts creators and industries by reducing revenue, and can lead to legal consequences for those involved.

CYBERSEX

A virtual sex experience in which two or more individuals (connected remotely through the internet) communicate each other sexually explicit contents or sexually explicit behaviors is known as computer sex, Internet sex, or netsex.

COMPUTER ADDICTION

Excessive computer use that interferes with day-to-day activities. Excessive usage can disrupt work or sleep, cause social interaction issues, and have an impact on mood, relationships, and thinking processes, among other things.

Digital Divide

The digital divide is the gap between people, communities, or countries that have access to modern technology and the internet, and those who don’t. It includes differences in access, digital skills, and the ability to use digital tools effectively

R.A. 10175

R.A. 10175, also known as the Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012, is a Philippine law designed to address cybercrime and enhance online security. It protects individuals and organizations from various digital threats while balancing concerns about free expression. The law covers offenses such as illegal access to computer systems, illegal interception of data, data interference, system interference, misuse of devices, cybersex for profit, child pornography, identity theft, and online libel.

Media Law and Regulation

Media law and regulation are rules that guide and control the media industry, focusing on content availability, age restrictions, ownership, and consumer protection. Laws on press freedom, intellectual property, privacy, and general civil and criminal law also affect media operations. In the Philippines, government bodies like the Movie and Television Review and Classification Board (MTRCB), the Optical Media Board, and the National Council for Children's Television oversee content regulation.
